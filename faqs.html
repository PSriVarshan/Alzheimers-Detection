Frequently Asked Questions

FAQs

What is AlzWELL?
AlzWELL is an AI-powered system designed for early detection and prediction of Alzheimer's Disease using MRI analysis, biomarkers, and advanced machine learning models.
How does MRI analysis work?
Our system processes DICOM-format MRI scans, converts them to image format, and uses a deep learning model to predict Alzheimer's based on image patterns.
What is biomarker analysis?
Biomarker analysis evaluates patient data such as age, gender, FDG and PIB values, marital status, and APOE4 allele count to predict Alzheimer's likelihood using an ensemble machine learning model.
Is the system suitable for clinical use?
While AlzWELL provides precise predictions, it is intended to assist researchers and clinicians rather than replace medical judgment.
How accurate is the system?
The accuracy depends on the quality of input data. Both MRI analysis and biomarker-based models are rigorously tested for high reliability and precision.
How does AlzWELL handle patient privacy?
AlzWELL ensures that patient data is anonymized and securely stored. It adheres to data protection regulations such as HIPAA and GDPR.
What formats are accepted for MRI scans?
The system primarily accepts MRI scans in DICOM (.dcm) format. These files are converted to image formats for processing by the deep learning model.
What is the role of APOE4 allele count in biomarker analysis?
APOE4 allele count is a key genetic factor associated with a higher risk of Alzheimer's disease. Our biomarker analysis integrates this information to improve prediction accuracy.
Can AlzWELL predict the progression of Alzheimer's disease?
Currently, AlzWELL focuses on early detection and prediction of Alzheimer's onset. Future versions aim to include progression tracking based on longitudinal data.
Does AlzWELL support multiple languages?
The current version of AlzWELL is available in English. Future updates may include multilingual support for global accessibility.
What are FDG and PIB values, and why are they important?
FDG (Fluorodeoxyglucose) and PIB (Pittsburgh Compound B) are imaging biomarkers used to study brain metabolism and amyloid plaque accumulation, respectively. They are critical indicators in Alzheimer's diagnosis.
How does the ensemble learning model improve prediction?
The ensemble learning model combines predictions from multiple algorithms like Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and SVM to improve overall accuracy and reduce bias in predictions.
Can the system be integrated with existing hospital management systems?
Yes, AlzWELL can be integrated with hospital management systems using APIs, enabling seamless data flow and improved diagnostics support.
What hardware requirements are needed to run AlzWELL?
AlzWELL is designed to work on systems with moderate computational resources. However, for large-scale MRI and biomarker analyses, a GPU-enabled system is recommended.
Is there a training period required to use the system?
The system is intuitive and user-friendly, requiring minimal training. A detailed user manual and tutorial videos are provided for ease of use.
How often should the models be updated?
To maintain accuracy and relevance, it is recommended to update the models annually or whenever significant new data becomes available.
What are the key benefits of using AlzWELL?
AlzWELL enables early detection of Alzheimer's, assists clinicians with data-driven insights, reduces diagnostic time, and improves patient care.
How does the system ensure unbiased predictions?
The models are trained on diverse and balanced datasets, and techniques like ensemble learning reduce the likelihood of algorithmic bias.
Can patients access AlzWELL directly?
AlzWELL is designed for use by medical professionals. Patients can benefit through their clinicians who use the system for diagnosis and recommendations.
